"it didn't start when i was little. it started in Cardiff."

My love for makeup, skincare and haircare didn't come from a childhood beauty-drawer moment. It came from university instead. Growing up, I used to learn makeup from my mum, watching her get ready. Now the roles have flipped where I'm the one teaching her, gifting her products or sending her links.

Then Space NK opened a store in Cardiff, and I remember the actual chitter chatter, the excitement flowing through my friend group before we'd even been inside. As soon as I went in, I was obsessed. Not with one product, but with the idea: hair care, perfumes, makeup, skincare, all luxurious, all under one roof.

That's where I found Rare Beauty and fell for the shade range on brown skin tones. That's where Living Proof's dry shampoo became a genuine holy grail for oily-hair-girl problems. That's also where Diptyque and Glossier stopped feeling out of reach and started actually being in my routine.

I love colourful makeup and minimal skincare; the loud and the quiet, existing on the same shelf. Products make me feel creative, and that's the whole reason I want to build campaigns for a brand that made me feel that way first.

04

campaign ideas for space nk

What I'd actually pitch: three concepts to start with.

the actual pitch 📌
concept 01

Mini-me: Dressing Your Makeup Bag for the Day

My makeup bag doesn't stay the same for two days in a row. It's college in the morning, a workout after that, office in the afternoon, a night out to close it off. mini-me builds that shift into an actual system: curated, travel-size kits organised by lifestyle moment, not by brand.

college → workout → office → night out, on loop ✦

60-SEC TIKTOK QUIZ  |  EDITS SHELF AT CHECKOUT  |  CROSS-BRAND DISCOVERY

concept 02

Left A Note

Let customers pick what's worth spotlighting, not just the brand. Anyone can leave a small handwritten note next to a product explaining why they love it, both in store and mirrored in the app. Whatever people are actually loving is what rises to the top.

The best notes get photographed and shared as their own bit of content, basically shelf wisdom from real people, sitting alongside the brand's own ads rather than replacing them. It gives the brand's own campaigns some real-world backup, proof from actual people sitting right next to the polished version.

CUSTOMER-WRITTEN, NOT BRAND-CURATED  |  SHELF NOTES → SHAREABLE CONTENT  |  TURNS WORD-OF-MOUTH INTO A SYSTEM

concept 03

Passed Down, Levelled Up

A gifting and content campaign built on the beauty handoff between generations: mum to daughter, or any pairing that fits. Small "duo" gift sets are designed to be shared between two people, not bought for just one, paired with UGC-style content of daughters introducing new products to their mums (and vice versa), real reactions, not scripted reviews.

TEACH MY MUM UGC  |  DUO GIFT SETS  |  REAL, NOT TREND-CHASING
